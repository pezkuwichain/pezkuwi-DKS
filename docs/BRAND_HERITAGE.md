# PezkuwiChain — Brand Heritage & Cultural Etymology

**The naming origins, etymology, and cultural foundations of the Digital Kurdistan State's blockchain.**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document records the cultural, linguistic, and symbolic foundations of PezkuwiChain — the
sovereign blockchain of the **Digital Kurdistan State (DKS)** — so that the meaning behind the
project's name, symbols, and visual identity is clear to the community, partners, and the public.

## 2. Etymology of "Pezkuwi"

### 2.1 Kurdish and its dialects

Kurdish is an Indo-Iranian language with two major dialect groups:

- **Kurmancî (Northern Kurdish)** — spoken in Bakur, Rojava, and much of the diaspora; written
  in the Hawar (Latin) alphabet.
- **Soranî (Central Kurdish)** — spoken in Başûr (Kurdistan Region of Iraq) and Rojhilat;
  written in a modified Arabic alphabet.

### 2.2 "Pez"

**"pez"** is one of the most ancient words in the Kurdish lexicon — small livestock, the
*Caprinae* family. It descends from the Avestan root *pasu-* ("sheep, domestic animal") and
belongs to the Proto-Indo-European *peḱu-* family, with cognates including Sanskrit *paśu-*,
Latin *pecus*, and German *Vieh*.

### 2.3 "Kûvî"

**"kûvî"** (also *kuwî*, *kîwî*) means wild, untamed, mountain-dwelling — used productively in
Kurdish to mark the wild counterpart of a domestic animal.

### 2.4 "Wild goat" across dialects

| Dialect | Compound | Composition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kurmancî** | *pezkûvî*, *bizina kûvî* | pez (livestock) + kûvî (wild) |
| **Soranî** | *bizinîkûvî*, *bizinîkîwî* | bizin (she-goat) + kûvî / kîwî (wild) |

Both refer to the same animal: *Capra aegagrus*, the bezoar wild goat. The difference is
purely dialectal.

### 2.5 The project name

**"Pezkuwi"** (from Kurmancî *pezkûvî*) is the public-facing brand — Kurmancî is the most widely
spoken Kurdish dialect and uses the Latin alphabet, well-suited to international branding. The
**Soranî** form appears in internal technical naming — e.g. **"Bizinikiwi"** (the core
framework, from *bizinîkîwî*) — acknowledging the dialect of the Kurdistan Region of Iraq where
the project is based. Both name the same animal: the wild goat, national totem of the Kurds.

### 2.6 Scientific & geographic note

The bezoar wild goat (*Capra aegagrus*) is the wild ancestor of the domestic goat. Its densest
populations occur in the **Zagros Mountains** of the Kurdistan Region of Iraq, extending into
eastern Turkey and western Iran. It was the first wild caprine domesticated by humans —
in the Zagros, ~10,000 years ago (Zeder & Hesse, 2000) — making it a foundational species in
the history of civilization, originating in the Kurdish homeland.

### 2.7 Cultural significance

In Kurdish tradition the *pezkuwi* is a **national totem animal**, paralleling the gray wolf for
Turkic peoples, the lion for Persians, or the eagle for many nations. It embodies **resilience**
(surviving harsh mountains), **freedom** (wild, never subjugated), and **connection to the
homeland**. As the saying goes, *"Kurdên çiyayî ne"* — "The Kurds are people of the mountain."

## 3. Naming of PezkuwiChain

- **"PezkuwiChain"** — the chain of the mountain goat; metaphorically, the chain of the
  mountain people.
- **"PEZ KIWI Company"** — the Latin-alphabet transliteration of *pez kûvî*, the project's
  registered company in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq.

The name reflects the mission: decentralized digital infrastructure for stateless peoples and
sovereign communities — for those whose freedom has always been tied to their mountains and to
their ability to govern themselves.

---

## 4. Visual Identity (summary)

The PezkuwiChain identity draws exclusively on Kurdish national iconography:

- **Central Roj (sun)** — from the flag of Kurdistan; its rays evoke *Newroz*.
- **Six petals** — the four regions of Kurdistan plus the global diaspora, in the organic
  rosette form of Kurdish textile and carpet tradition.
- **Kesk / Sor / Zer** — the green, red, and yellow of the Kurdistan flag.

## 5. Independence & Open-Source Foundation

PezkuwiChain is an **independent** Kurdish project, not affiliated with or endorsed by any other
blockchain project or foundation. Its software is built upon the open-source **polkadot-sdk**,
used under the **Apache-2.0** and **GPL-3.0** licenses, which expressly permit forking,
modification, and redistribution. Attribution to the original authors is provided in the
repository [`NOTICE`](../NOTICE) file.

The name "Pezkuwi" is a Kurdish word with its own language of origin, etymology, meaning, and
cultural reference frame — a coherent native term (with a documented Soranî counterpart,
*bizinîkîwî*), independent in origin and meaning.

## 6. Concluding Statement

PezkuwiChain draws its name from the Kurdish language, its symbols from Kurdish national
iconography, its colors from the Kurdish flag, and its mission from the Kurdish people's
long experience of building resilient communities in defiance of external power. It is a
Kurdish project — built for the Kurdish nation and for all peoples who share its values of
sovereignty, freedom, and self-determination.
